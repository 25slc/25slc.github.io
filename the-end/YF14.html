Youth Faculty 14: Safety

Under Youth Assembly 5: School Climate

Hello, We are Youth Faculty 14, and the issue we are discussing is Safety in School Climate. School climate refers to the quality of school life. It is important to have a safe school climate for students to study in, as it allows students to be in a more conducive environment that promotes social and creative learning and they would be confident and feel safe in their school environment, making it like their second home.

Threats to Safety in School Climate are categorised into two aspects, Physical and Mental. Physical threats include school shootings unsafe infrastructure (labarotories, rooms), unpreparedness in times of crisis (lack of planning of fire and lockdown drills), and terrorist attacks (Beslan school siege). Mental threats include bullying (Verbal Bullying, Cyber Bullying), stress (stress-induced self-harm, examinations, suicidal thoughts), communications & relations with schools/ teachers (The use of tone and language) and stigmatisation (Elite schools vs Neighbourhood Schools, Streaming, Different family backgrounds)

We have decided to focus on Bullying, and it is defined as an act of repeated aggression with intentions to hurt another person, physically or mentally. It can also be characterized as an individual behaving in a certain way to gain power over another person. Bullying is further broken down into three forms: physical, mental and cyber (as we recognise that in the 21st century, with the advent of technology, acts of bullying can take place online as well).
